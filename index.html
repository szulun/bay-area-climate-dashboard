Policy Simulator — What if your county adopted these policies?
Toggle policies on/off to see projected 2030 emissions impact and the tradeoffs each policy creates.
Simulating for:
Reach Code — Building Electrification
Ban new gas appliances → -4% GHG from buildings by 2030
✅ Eliminates gas bills long-term · improves indoor air quality · lowers fire risk
⚠️ Higher upfront appliance costs · requires electrical upgrades · hardship for renters
EV Charging Expansion — 30% coverage
Accelerate EV adoption to 35% by 2030 → -6% transport GHG
✅ Reduces gas costs · lowers tailpipe pollution near schools · grid-flexible charging
⚠️ Chargers can displace street parking · benefits skew to higher incomes · grid load increase
Public Transit Frequency Boost
+40% BART/bus frequency → -3% VMT, -2% transport GHG
✅ Benefits lower-income riders most · reduces road congestion · catalyzes density
⚠️ High operating costs · may require reducing car lanes · requires sustained funding
Community Solar Program
Subsidize rooftop solar for low-income homes → -5% energy GHG
✅ Addresses EJ gap · lowers energy bills · builds community wealth
⚠️ Complex administration · requires renters/landlord coordination · grid interconnection delays
Cool Roofs Mandate (Dr. Vahmani)
White rooftops reduce waste heat → -3% urban heat, -2% AC energy
✅ Reduces heat island effect · lowers AC bills · benefits vulnerable neighborhoods most
⚠️ Compliance enforcement difficult · some aesthetic objections · upfront retrofit costs
